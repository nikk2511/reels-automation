# Format Winners

Last updated: 2026-03-14

## Formats to prioritize

### 1. Fast "3 updates in 20 seconds" roundup

- Why it fits: maps to current verified launch cadence and supports saves/shares.
- Structure:
  - 0-2s hook with a strong promise
  - 2-15s one sentence per update
  - 15-22s "which one do you want tested next?" CTA

### 2. One tool, one workflow, one visible outcome

- Why it fits: easier to produce in text-only mode and stronger for credibility than broad lists.
- Structure:
  - pain point
  - tool reveal
  - 2 or 3 concrete use cases
  - save/follow CTA

### 3. Before/after workflow reframing

- Why it fits: good for AI tools because the "before" is often obvious wasted effort.
- Structure:
  - before: manual process
  - after: AI-assisted process
  - caveat: what the tool still does not replace

## Hook patterns to reuse

- "Three AI launches this week that actually matter."
- "If you use AI for work, this changed fast."
- "Most people missed what this AI update actually does."
- "This is not just another AI video tool."
- "Before you pay for another AI app, look at this."

## Editing rules

- Show the payoff before details whenever possible.
- Put the tool name in the first frame.
- Use captions throughout; many viewers watch muted.
- Keep cuts dense and purposeful; no branded intro before the hook.
- Use original voiceover or clean native audio.

## Constraints for this automation

- Media generation tools unavailable: default to text-first scripts, shot lists, caption overlays, and posting instructions.
- Posting and analytics tools unavailable: do not claim results; provide manual posting checklist instead.

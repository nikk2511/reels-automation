# Lessons Learned

Last updated: 2026-03-14

## Current operating rules

- Build around verified launches and official product updates; avoid recycled "top AI tools" lists unless they add distribution context.
- Favor original narration, native editing, clean audio, and no third-party watermarks to stay aligned with Instagram recommendation guidance relayed from Adam Mosseri coverage.
- Treat hashtags as support metadata, not the core growth lever; lead with on-screen keywords and captions that clearly name the tool and use case.
- Prioritize save/share formats over generic inspiration. For an AI-tools niche account, "what changed this week" and "tool + real use case" concepts are stronger than vague productivity motivation.
- Keep most educational Reels in the 15-30 second range unless the demo genuinely needs more time.

## What to avoid

- Invented performance claims, fake social proof, or "everyone is using this" language without a source.
- Broad tool roundups with no point of view. A Reel needs one clear promise: new launch, specific workflow, or strong comparison.
- Hard-selling captions. The current recommendation environment rewards usefulness and originality more than obvious promotion.

## Signals to reuse next run

  - OpenAI `GPT-5.4` launch on 2026-03-05
  - Runway `Characters` launch on 2026-03-09
